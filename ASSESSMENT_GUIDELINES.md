# 📝 Assessment Guidelines

Assessments are an essential component of the **1-Month Bioinformatics Bootcamp 2026**. They are designed to strengthen conceptual understanding, encourage scientific reasoning, and help participants apply the knowledge gained during the lectures.

---

## Assessment Objectives

The assessments aim to evaluate:

- Conceptual understanding
- Critical thinking
- Scientific reasoning
- Problem-solving ability
- Communication of biological concepts

---

## Submission Format

- Submit assessments as a **single PDF file** unless otherwise instructed.
- Ensure that all responses are clear, well-organized, and readable.

---

## Original Work

Participants must submit their own work.

Responses should:

- Be written in your own words.
- Demonstrate your understanding of the concepts.
- Include relevant examples where appropriate.

Plagiarism and excessive reliance on AI-generated content are discouraged and may affect evaluation.

---

## Submission Deadline

Each assessment will have its own submission deadline.

Late submissions may not be considered unless prior approval has been obtained.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Assessments will be reviewed based on:

- Accuracy
- Conceptual understanding
- Critical analysis
- Logical reasoning
- Clarity of explanation
- Originality

---

## Outstanding Submissions

Exceptional assessments may be featured on the official **BioCode Innovators** platforms and GitHub repository to recognize academic excellence and inspire future learners.

---

## Feedback

Where applicable, feedback will be provided to help participants improve their understanding and strengthen future submissions.

---

## Learning First

The purpose of these assessments is not simply to obtain a score but to reinforce learning, encourage curiosity, and develop a deeper understanding of Bioinformatics.

We encourage every participant to approach each assessment with sincerity, curiosity, and a willingness to learn.

**Best wishes, and happy learning! 🧬**
