# 🧬 Lecture 04 – Data Retrieval from Biological Databases

This lecture focused on the practical process of retrieving biological data from major public databases. Participants learned how to search, access, and interpret biological information from **NCBI**, **UniProt**, and the **Protein Data Bank (PDB)**, which are essential resources in modern bioinformatics research.

## 📚 Topics Covered

- Introduction to Biological Data Retrieval
- Navigating the NCBI Database
- Retrieving DNA and Protein Sequences from NCBI
- Exploring UniProt for Protein Information
- Accessing Protein Structures from the Protein Data Bank (PDB)
- Understanding Accession IDs and Database Records
- Downloading Biological Data in Different File Formats (FASTA, GenBank, PDB, XML, TXT)
- Best Practices for Biological Data Retrieval and Documentation

By the end of this lecture, participants were able to retrieve biological sequences and protein structures from major databases, understand different data formats, and recognize the importance of these resources in biological and computational research.
