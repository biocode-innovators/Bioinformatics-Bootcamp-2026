# 🧬 Lecture 03 – Protein Databases

This lecture introduced participants to **Protein Databases**, one of the fundamental resources in Bioinformatics for storing, organizing, and retrieving protein-related information. We explored how researchers use these databases to investigate protein sequences, structures, functions, and biological annotations.

## 📚 Topics Covered

- Introduction to Protein Databases
- Importance of Protein Databases in Biological Research
- UniProt: Protein Sequence & Functional Information
- Protein Data Bank (PDB): Protein Structure Repository
- Types of Protein Data and Annotations
- Protein Accession IDs and Database Records
- Applications of Protein Databases in Bioinformatics Research

By the end of this lecture, participants gained a foundational understanding of how protein databases support biological research and how these resources are used to study protein function, structure, and evolution.
